Introduction: The Flaming Cliffs Experience

Nestled in the heart of Mongolia's Gobi Desert, the Flaming Cliffs—known locally as Bayanzag—beckon adventurers from around the globe. Their fiery red hues, carved by time and wind, are a stunning testament to the power of nature. Famous as the cradle of dinosaur discoveries, this breathtaking landscape whispers stories of ancient worlds and invites you to create unforgettable memories.


From sunrise to sunset, the Flaming Cliffs radiate an otherworldly beauty that captures the imagination. Step into a land of discovery, where history and natural splendor converge in an awe-inspiring experience.

Gateway to Prehistory

The Flaming Cliffs gained global fame in the 1920s when paleontologist Roy Chapman Andrews unearthed the first dinosaur eggs here. This discovery redefined our understanding of prehistoric life and placed the Flaming Cliffs firmly on the map of world heritage. Today, the site stands as a living museum, connecting us to a time when dinosaurs roamed the earth.

Every visitor who steps foot here walks in the footsteps of history, experiencing the thrill of standing where groundbreaking discoveries were made. As you explore the cliffs, you’ll uncover layers of geological and cultural significance that make this place truly unique.

Entry Fees & Preservation

Visitors to the Flaming Cliffs contribute directly to preserving this historic site. Entry fees ensure ongoing research, conservation efforts, and public education:

  • Domestic Visitors:3,000 MNT
  • Tourists: 10,000 MNT

Your visit also directly contributes to the preservation and enhancement of this iconic site. Every ticket sold supports essential projects such as:

  • Stair and pathway renovations
  • Regular cleaning and maintenance
  • Eco-friendly fencing that accommodates herders and livestock
  • Planting trees to combat desertification

Meet the dedicated team working tirelessly to protect and promote the Flaming Cliffs. Their commitment ensures this destination remains pristine and accessible for generations to come.

Key Fossils & Paleontological Discoveries

Discovering Dinosaurs

The Flaming Cliffs are one of the richest fossil sites in the world. In the 1920s, Roy Chapman Andrews and his team discovered some of the most iconic dinosaur fossils here.

•

Velociraptor: Famous for its speed and agility, Velociraptor fossils, including one locked in combat with Protoceratops, were first unearthed here.

•

Protoceratops: Known as the "sheep of the Cretaceous," Protoceratops fossils offer insights into herd behavior and nesting habits.

•

Dinosaur Eggs: Andrews’ groundbreaking discovery of the first dinosaur eggs revolutionized our understanding of dinosaur reproduction.
