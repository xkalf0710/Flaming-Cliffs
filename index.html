The Flaming Cliffs Experience

Nestled in the heart of Mongolia's Gobi Desert, the Flaming Cliffs beckon adventurers from around the globe. Their fiery red hues, carved by time and wind, are a stunning testament to the power of nature. Famous as the cradle of dinosaur discoveries, this breathtaking landscape whispers stories of ancient worlds and invites you to create unforgettable memories.

From sunrise to sunset, the Flaming Cliffs radiate an otherworldly beauty that captures the imagination. Step into a land of discovery, where history and natural splendor converge in an awe-inspiring experience.

Entry Fees & Preservation

Visitors to the Flaming Cliffs contribute directly to preserving this historic site. Entry fees ensure ongoing research, conservation efforts, and public education:

  • Domestic Visitors: 3,000 MNT
  • Tourists: 10,000 MNT

Your visit also directly contributes to the preservation and enhancement of this iconic site. Every ticket sold supports essential projects such as:

  • Stair and pathway renovations
  • Regular cleaning and maintenance
  • Eco-friendly fencing that accommodates herders and livestock
  • Planting trees to combat desertification

Meet the dedicated team working tirelessly to protect and promote the Flaming Cliffs. Their commitment ensures this destination remains pristine and accessible for generations to come.

Getting There

Located in the Gobi Desert, the Flaming Cliffs are about 120 kilometers from Dalanzadgad. Options to reach the site include:

  • Self-Drive: A 4×4 vehicle is recommended, but locals often use standard vehicles depending on rainfall.
  • Guided Tours: Day trips or multi-day expeditions.
  • Flights: Domestic flights to Dalanzadgad, followed by a drive to the site.

Best Time To Visit

The ideal months for visiting are May through October, with mild weather and accessible roads.

Visitor Guidelines

  • Stay on designated paths to avoid disturbing fossils.
  • Fossil collection is strictly prohibited.
  • Wear sturdy shoes, sun protection, and carry water.

Frequently asked questions

1. When is the best time to visit?
May to October, during mild weather and accessible road conditions.
2. Can I collect fossils?
No, fossil collection is prohibited to ensure preservation for future generations.
3. Are there nearby accommodations?
Yes, options include traditional ger camps and modern hotels in Dalanzadgad.
4. What transportation options are available?
Self-drive, guided tours, and flights to Dalanzadgad followed by a drive.
5. What should I bring for my visit?
Sturdy shoes, sun protection, plenty of water, and a sense of adventure.
6. Is there food available at the Flaming Cliffs?
Yes, the Information Center offers a dining area with local and international cuisine.
